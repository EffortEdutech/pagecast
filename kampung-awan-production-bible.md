## 1. Franchise Identity

**Franchise Name:** Kampung Awan Club

**Tagline / Golden Rule:** *"Small magic. Big heart."*

**Alternative Subtitles:**
- Adventures Above the Clouds
- Tales from the Sky Village
- The Hidden Village in the Clouds

**Genre:** Whimsical Southeast Asian-inspired fantasy comedy

**Emotional DNA:** Every story should feel cozy, playful, safe, imaginative, and emotionally warm. Children should finish each episode feeling happy, comforted, curious, and emotionally understood.

**Format:** Standalone episodic stories set in the same universe. No heavy mythology or continuity dependency — every entry point is a low barrier.

## 3. World Summary & Rules

### The Premise

Far above the human world floats a hidden magical village: **Kampung Awan**. Humans rarely see it because clouds hide it, magic bends perception, and adults stop noticing magical things. Children can sometimes discover it accidentally.

### World Rules

**Rule 1 — Magic Is Imperfect**
Magic malfunctions, misunderstands instructions, and creates funny consequences. Magic must never feel overpowered.

**Rule 2 — No True Villains**
Conflicts come from misunderstandings, accidents, fears, curiosity, and magical chaos. No evil warlords or dark violence.

**Rule 3 — Small Problems Feel Big**
A lost pancake recipe, a runaway cloud bicycle, an invisible goat eating laundry — for children, these ARE huge adventures.

**Rule 4 — Kindness Solves More Than Strength**
Solutions involve teamwork, empathy, creativity, honesty, and emotional understanding. Not fighting.

**Rule 5 — Wonder Is Ordinary**
Residents treat magical things casually. Flying fish at breakfast, noodle dragons delivering mail, talking umbrellas. This makes the world feel alive.

## 4. Character Bible

### Main Core Cast

#### 1. Nino
- **Type:** Human boy, age 8
- **Role:** Overconfident "leader"
- **Personality:** Confident, dramatic, imaginative, terrible at planning
- **Comedy Engine:** Acts first, thinks later
- **Catchphrase:** *"I totally know what I'm doing."* (Usually does not.)
- **Signature Item:** Oversized explorer goggles
- **Weakness:** Cannot whisper
- **Speech Pattern:** Confident exaggeration — *"This is definitely part of the plan."*

#### 2. Mimi
- **Type:** Human girl
- **Role:** Quiet observer, the real problem solver
- **Personality:** Calm, clever, emotionally mature, secretly competitive
- **Comedy Engine:** Always right. Nobody listens until disaster happens.
- **Signature Item:** Tiny notebook full of strange sketches
- **Speech Pattern:** Short smart observations — *"Maybe we should not press that."*

#### 3. Cici
- **Type:** Inventor girl
- **Personality:** Chaotic genius, hyperactive, optimistic
- **Comedy Engine:** Builds inventions that almost work
- **Signature Gadgets:** Pancake drone, bubble shoes, automatic mosquito fan, noodle-powered fan, slipper-catapult, automatic pancake helmet
- **Visual:** Messy toolbelt and oversized gloves

#### 4. Bubu
- **Type:** Cloud creature — primary mascot
- **Personality:** Emotional, loyal, easily panics
- **Comedy Engine:** Transforms into random objects when stressed (toaster, umbrella, chicken, traffic cone, vacuum cleaner, electric drill)
- **Speech:** Simple emotional sounds — *"Buuuu…"*
- **Merch Potential:** Extremely high

#### 5. Pak Tulo
- **Type:** Tiny magical grandfather spirit
- **Personality:** Wise-sounding, forgetful, dramatic storyteller
- **Comedy Engine:** Confidently gives wrong advice
- **Signature Item:** Flying teacup
- **Speech Pattern:** Dramatic storytelling — *"Ah yes… this reminds me of the Great Pancake Disaster…"*

## 5. Story Bible

### Visual Identity

**Art Style Direction:**
- Storybook fantasy
- Soft lighting, rounded shapes, expressive faces
- Cozy clutter, warm atmosphere
- Texture should feel handmade, soft, touchable, slightly imperfect
- Avoid: sterile sci-fi look, hyper-realism, sharp aggressive designs

**Visual Inspiration:** Indonesian street warmth, Ghibli coziness, cartoon absurdity, pastel fantasy

### Color System

**Daytime Palette:** Warm sky blues, pastel orange, creamy yellow, leaf green, cotton white

**Night Palette:** Moon purple, deep blue, lantern gold, sleepy teal

**Emotional Color Language:**
- Chaos: bright saturated colors
- Sadness: rainy blues and soft gray
- Wonder: glowing gold and pink

---

| The Cloud Forest | Soft floating trees. Creatures hide here. |

### Humor Rules

**Primary Humor Types:**
- Visual comedy
- Misunderstandings
- Exaggerated reactions
- Absurd magical logic

**Never Use:**
- Cruelty
- Humiliation
- Sarcasm-heavy jokes
- Adult cynicism
- Edgy humor

**Good Humor Examples:**
- Goat accidentally becoming mayor
- Noodle monster scared of noodles
- Invisible raincloud following one child only
- Dragon sneezing fireworks indoors

**Humor Style:** Chaotic, visual, warm, absurd but safe. Inspired by gentle anime comedy, classic cartoon timing, Indonesian/Malaysian neighborhood warmth, bedtime silliness.

---

### Dialogue Style

- Must sound natural, playful, emotionally simple, memorable
- No complex vocabulary for primary audience

**Character Speech Patterns:**
| Character | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Nino | Confident exaggeration | *"This is definitely part of the plan."* |
| Mimi | Short smart observations | *"Maybe we should not press that."* |
| Pak Tulo | Dramatic storytelling | *"Ah yes… this reminds me of the Great Pancake Disaster…"* |
| Bubu | Simple emotional sounds | *"Buuuu…"* |

---

### Episode Structure

**Standard 4-Act Formula:**

- **ACT 1 — Discovery:** Something strange happens.
- **ACT 2 — Chaos:** Attempts to fix it make things worse.
- **ACT 3 — Emotional Realization:** Characters understand the real problem.
- **ACT 4 — Cozy Resolution:** Problem solved creatively. Warm ending scene.

---

### Episode Length System

| Format | Word Count | Age Target |
|---|---|---|
| Mini Stories | 400–700 words | Ages 5–6 |
| Standard Stories | 800–1,500 words | Ages 6–9 |
| Special Episodes | 2,000–3,000 words | All ages, rare, more emotional |

**Read time:** 5–10 minutes for Standard Stories. Each story is standalone.

---

### Emotional Lesson Rules

Lessons must **never feel preachy**. They should emerge naturally from consequences, feelings, and friendship.

**Allowed Themes:** Honesty, teamwork, patience, courage, empathy, responsibility, creativity, forgiveness

**Forbidden Themes:** Hopelessness, nihilism, revenge, excessive fear, graphic sadness

---

### Creature Design Rules

Every creature must have:
1. One visual gimmick
2. One emotional gimmick
3. One comedy weakness

Silhouette must be recognizable instantly. Creatures should be drawable by children — very important.

---

### Music & Audio Direction

**Music Style:** Playful orchestral, toy instruments, ukulele, soft gamelan inspiration, whistles, hand percussion

**SFX Philosophy:** Sound effects should feel bouncy, exaggerated, cartoony, satisfying

---

### PageCast Adaptation Formats

| Format | Focus |
|---|---|
| Text Version | Bedtime readable |
| Motion Comic Version | Simple camera pans and animated effects |
| TikTok Version | Visual chaos, punchline moments, mascot reactions |
| Audio Story Version | Strong creature sounds and expressive narration |

---

### Continuity Rules

**Soft Continuity:** Episodes stand alone. Recurring memories are allowed.

**Hard Continuity — Avoid:**
- Complex lore dependency
- Dark mythology
- Heavy timelines

Keep entry barrier low at all times.

## 7. Sample Story

### Bubu and the Very Unfortunate Festival Problem

*A Kampung Awan Story — Standard Episode, ~1,200 words*
*Emotional Theme: Embarrassment and Acceptance*

---

The lantern festival only happened once every year in Kampung Awan.

It was the happiest night above the clouds.

Tiny glowing lanterns floated through the air like sleepy fireflies. The snack stalls filled the village square with delicious smells of grilled bananas, sweet pancakes, and spicy noodles. Music bounced through the sky while children ran across the bridges laughing.

And this year, Bubu was determined to help.

"I can do festival jobs too," Bubu said proudly.

"Buuuu!"

Bubu puffed up happily like a fluffy marshmallow cloud.

Nino adjusted his oversized explorer goggles dramatically.

"Excellent," he announced. "Every great festival team needs a professional assistant."

"You are not a professional anything," Mimi said quietly.

Cici rolled a cart full of strange inventions into the square.

"I made automatic lantern polishers!" she said.

The machine immediately exploded into bubbles.

Everyone ducked.

Pak Tulo floated by in his flying teacup.

"Ah yes," he said wisely. "A successful invention always explodes at least once."

"That doesn't sound right," Mimi muttered.

Mak Cempaka clapped her hands loudly from her floating snack stall.

"Festival starts in one hour!" she shouted. "Please try not to destroy the village before then!"

Everyone nodded seriously.

Unfortunately, this was already becoming difficult.

---

Bubu's first job was simple: carry lantern ribbons.

"That's all?" Bubu asked.

"Very important ribbons," Nino said.

Bubu proudly floated across the square carrying a giant stack of colorful ribbons.

Then a loud BOOOOM! echoed nearby.

Cici's bubble machine launched itself into the sky.

Bubu jumped in surprise.

POOF.

Suddenly, Bubu transformed into… a toaster.

A small pink toaster with blinking eyes.

The ribbons fell everywhere.

Nino stared. Mimi blinked once.

The toaster popped out two slices of toast nervously.

"Buu…"

"Oh no," said Mimi.

---

Bubu transformed whenever Bubu got nervous. Usually it lasted a few seconds. This time it did not.

Nino picked up the toaster carefully.

"You know," he said, "this is probably fine."

The toaster shot toast directly into his face.

They hurried behind Mak Cempaka's stall.

"Okay," Cici said. "Don't panic."

The toaster vibrated anxiously.

POP. More toast flew into the air.

"Bubu," Mimi said gently, "you just need to calm down."

Easy to say. The festival square was becoming louder every minute. Lanterns floated overhead. Drums played nearby. Children raced through the streets.

The toaster began shaking harder.

POOF.

Now Bubu was a standing fan.

The fan immediately spun at maximum speed. Napkins blasted across the square. Three hats flew away. Pak Tulo's beard whipped around like a frightened squirrel.

"MY FACE IS EXPERIENCING WEATHER!" he shouted.

Nino grabbed the fan. The fan spun harder. Nino slid backward across the bridge.

"WHY IS IT SO STRONG?!" he yelled.

---

Soon everyone gathered for the opening parade. Mak Cempaka carried glowing cloud pancakes. Uncle Garu dragged lantern poles tied together entirely with rope. Sulu the thundercloud floated sleepily overhead, wearing a tiny festival hat.

Everything looked magical. Everything looked perfect.

Then Bubu accidentally turned into a vacuum cleaner. Right in the middle of the parade.

WHOOOOOOOOSH.

The vacuum immediately sucked up flower petals, festival maps, someone's scarf, and Uncle Garu's shoe.

"MY GOOD SHOE!" Uncle Garu cried.

Children screamed with laughter. The vacuum rolled wildly through the square. Nino chased after it. Cici crashed into noodle crates. Mimi somehow caught three lanterns at once.

The vacuum bumped into Pak Tulo.

WHOOP.

Pak Tulo's hat disappeared inside.

"My wisdom hat!" he gasped.

The vacuum hiccuped. The hat popped back out covered in glitter.

Everyone laughed. Except Bubu. The vacuum cleaner slowly drooped sadly.

"Buu…"

Mimi noticed immediately.

"Oh," she whispered.

Bubu was embarrassed.

---

They finally cornered the runaway vacuum near the noodle alley. Cici unplugged it dramatically even though it had no cord. The vacuum transformed back into fluffy little Bubu.

But Bubu looked miserable. Tiny rainclouds floated over Bubu's head.

"I ruin everything," Bubu mumbled.

"You absolutely do not," said Nino.

"I made the parade messy."

"You made it exciting," said Pak Tulo proudly, still covered in glitter.

"But everyone laughed."

Mimi sat beside Bubu quietly.

"They laughed because it was funny," she said softly. "Not because they hate you."

Bubu looked unconvinced.

Across the village square, children were already pretending to be runaway vacuum cleaners and crashing into each other dramatically.

One tiny boy shouted: "LOOK! I'M BUBU!"

The other children giggled happily.

Bubu blinked.

"They're copying me?"

"You are currently very popular," said Mimi.

---

Mak Cempaka suddenly hurried over looking panicked.

"Problem!" she cried. Everyone froze.

"The lantern platform is stuck!"

At the center of the village stood the giant festival lantern tower. Every year, it lifted hundreds of glowing lanterns into the sky together. But now the wooden platform would not move.

Uncle Garu pulled on ropes. Nothing. Cici hit a lever with a spoon. Still nothing.

"No lantern launch means no festival ending," Mak Cempaka said sadly.

Then Bubu had an idea. A very strange idea.

"Maybe…" Bubu said slowly, "maybe I can help."

Nino gasped dramatically. "A hero moment."

"Please stop calling everything a hero moment," Mimi said.

Bubu floated toward the lantern tower. "I need to get nervous on purpose."

"That sounds dangerous," said Cici.

Bubu nodded. "Probably."

---

Bubu took a deep breath. The whole village watched.

"Okay," Bubu whispered. "Think scary thoughts."

Bubu squeezed eyes shut.

"What if everyone sees me dance badly?" — POOF. Bubu became a blender.

"Not useful yet," Mimi said.

"What if I forget everyone's birthday forever?" — POOF. Now Bubu was a floor lamp.

Children clapped anyway.

"Closer!" shouted Nino.

Bubu concentrated harder.

"What if I accidentally call Mak Cempaka 'Grandma Pancake' again?"

"YOU DID WHAT?" Mak Cempaka yelled.

POOF!

Suddenly Bubu transformed into a giant electric drill.

Cici's eyes sparkled. "Oh this is AMAZING."

The drill buzzed loudly. Uncle Garu pointed at the jammed platform gears. "THAT! DRILL THAT!"

The giant drill bounced nervously toward the platform.

BRRRRRRRRRRRR. Sparks flew. The gears shook. The platform creaked once.

Then— FWOOOOOSH!

The lantern tower burst to life. Hundreds of glowing lanterns floated upward into the night sky.

The entire village cheered. Golden lights drifted above the clouds like floating stars.

Bubu transformed back midair with a surprised squeak. "Buuuu?!"

Nino caught Bubu dramatically. "I SAVED YOU," he declared.

"You fell two centimeters," Mimi said.

---

The festival became even more lively afterward. Children chased lantern reflections. Music echoed across the bridges. Even Sulu woke up long enough to clap twice before falling asleep again.

And everywhere Bubu looked, people were smiling. Not angry. Not annoyed. Happy.

One little girl approached shyly.

"Can you turn into a toaster again?" she asked.

Bubu blinked. "Why?"

The girl grinned. "It was funny."

Soon more children gathered.

"Become the vacuum!"
"No, the fan!"
"TOASTER!"

Bubu looked nervous again. POOF. A toaster appeared. POP! Toast launched directly into Nino's face again.

Everyone exploded with laughter. Even Bubu. Especially Bubu.

---

Later that night, the friends sat together near the lantern bridge eating cloud pancakes. The sky glowed softly above them.

"I still get embarrassed," Bubu admitted quietly.

Mimi nodded. "That's normal."

"Even for magical cloud creatures?"

"Especially for magical cloud creatures," said Pak Tulo wisely.

Then he accidentally sat in syrup. Nobody told him immediately.

Nino pointed at the sky dramatically. "Tonight shall be remembered forever!"

"Why?" asked Cici.

"Because," Nino announced proudly, "this was the first festival ever defeated by household appliances."

Everyone laughed so hard that even the lanterns seemed to wobble.

And beside them, fluffy little Bubu smiled happily. Exactly as Bubu was.

## 8. PageCast Content Hierarchy

### The Brand Decision

After evaluating all naming options, the following two-tier system is the official PageCast content vocabulary:

| Level | Term | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Platform | **PageCast** | The platform itself |
| Product Line | **Collection** | A themed group of Castlets under one universe or franchise |
| Individual Unit | **Castlet** | A single story experience |

---

### Why "Castlet"

The term *Castlet* works because it is:
- Lighter than "episode"
- More collectible than "story"
- Broader than "book"
- More proprietary than "chapter"
- Naturally connected to PageCast

**Usage example:** *"I bought 3 new Castlets today."*

That's the ecosystem language strong platforms build.

---

### Why "Collection"

*Collection* is universal, premium, scalable, genre-neutral, and internationally understood. It replaces generic terms like "pack," "bundle," or "series."

---

### Storefront Naming Format

```
[Collection Name] — [N] Castlets Collection
```

**Examples:**
- Kampung Awan Club — 5 Castlets Collection
- Dream Forest — 8 Castlets Collection
- Tiny Detectives — 12 Castlets Collection

**Most Premium Format:**
> The Kampung Awan Club Collection
> Volume 1 • 5 Castlets
